# 004-transfer: Feature Parking Lot

Context Missions are the selected feature direction for `004-transfer`. The ideas below are intentionally deferred: they are not rejected, scheduled, or part of the Context Missions V1 scope. Reconsider an idea only when its revisit signal becomes true and a later product decision activates it.

The earlier LearnLoop parking lot remains at [002-learnloop-feature-parking-lot.md](./002-learnloop-feature-parking-lot.md).

## Pattern Lab and sentence coaching

Explain Dutch word order, separable verbs, idioms, and sentence construction, then provide substitution or reordering exercises around the pattern.

**Why parked:** Context Missions first need to prove that short recall and reconstruction exercises improve transfer. A broader coach could become an unbounded explanation product and would require dependable linguistic analysis.

**Revisit when:** Context Missions are stable and learner evidence shows that sentence structure, rather than vocabulary or task comprehension, is the next recurring barrier.

## Can-do Passport

Track practical accomplishments such as understanding a disruption notice, extracting a deadline from a municipality letter, or choosing an appropriate response.

**Why parked:** Credible can-do progression requires validated task definitions and evidence rules. Mission completion alone must not be presented as proof of real-world capability.

**Revisit when:** Several Context Mission task types have been tested with learners and can be mapped responsibly to practical outcomes.

## Personalized remix lessons

Reuse selected learning items inside stable, reviewed lesson templates.

**Why parked:** Personalization needs selection, difficulty, sequencing, and fallback rules. It should not distract from validating webpage-anchored practice or introduce unconstrained generated lessons.

**Revisit when:** Context Missions are learning-validated and enough learner-approved vocabulary exists to personalize reviewed templates without reducing quality.

## Pronunciation and listening

Add Dutch audio, listening recall, learner recording, shadowing, pronunciation practice, or pronunciation feedback.

**Why parked:** Context Missions V1 explicitly excludes voice and audio. These capabilities introduce audio sourcing, speech capture, feedback quality, privacy, permission, and cross-browser questions.

**Revisit when:** Context Missions are validated, learners identify listening or pronunciation as the highest-value transfer gap, and DutchMate has approved audio-source, privacy, and feedback-quality plans.

## Narrow reading trails

Offer several related Dutch headlines or short texts that deliberately repeat useful vocabulary and patterns around one topic.

**Why parked:** The selected feature should first prove learning value on webpages learners already choose. Curated trails add source selection, freshness, licensing, difficulty control, and continuing editorial maintenance.

**Revisit when:** Context Missions work reliably across learner-chosen pages and users show that finding appropriately related Dutch input is a recurring obstacle.

## Daily Dutch brief

Curate current Dutch headlines and turn them into prediction, comprehension, and retrieval activities rather than a passive news feed.

**Why parked:** A headline feed alone does not meet the real-world-transfer bar. An instructional brief adds news freshness, source reliability, rights, moderation, and recurring editorial work.

**Revisit when:** DutchMate has a sustainable current-content process and evidence that learners want DutchMate to choose daily reading material.

## Pragmatics and culture decoder

Teach modal particles, directness, formal and informal address, letter conventions, and expressions whose literal translation hides their social meaning.

**Why parked:** This needs expert cultural and linguistic review and more nuance than deterministic V1 missions should claim to derive from arbitrary webpages.

**Revisit when:** Learner feedback repeatedly identifies pragmatic misunderstanding as a transfer barrier and DutchMate has an expert-reviewed authoring process.

## Cultural media lab

Use Dutch sayings, quotations, short poems, or licensed song excerpts for figurative meaning, rhythm, cultural context, and deliberate reuse.

**Why parked:** This is less central to the A0–A2 first audience than practical webpage transfer. It also introduces attribution, translation review, licensing, and possibly audio-rights obligations.

**Revisit when:** Practical Context Missions are validated, learners ask for cultural or literary depth, and each item has a clear learning task plus verified reuse rights.

## AI-generated mission expansion

Use an LLM to generate explanations, distractors, comprehension questions, or practical response tasks from arbitrary webpage text.

**Why parked:** It would break the deterministic, zero-incremental-request V1 boundary and introduce cost, latency, prompt safety, quality evaluation, privacy, and failure-mode concerns.

**Revisit when:** Deterministic missions are validated, a specific valuable task cannot be produced locally, and an explicit provider, budget, privacy, and quality contract is approved.

## Mission history and popup destination

Add mission history, resumable missions, a Today card, or a dedicated Missions tab.

**Why parked:** One 20–45-second webpage exercise does not justify another navigation destination or persistent queue. These surfaces would create activity-oriented progress and popup complexity before learners show a need for them.

**Revisit when:** Learners repeatedly lose valuable work because missions cannot resume, or a validated multi-session mission workflow needs a durable home.
