Recommendation

Choose A: Daily Brief + compact library.

It keeps Daily Five genuinely primary, treats learning history as supporting evidence, and turns twelve large lesson cards into a readable index.

  • Daily Five: keep one primary action. “Review more” belongs after completion or in a quiet secondary action—not alongside the first decision.
  • Learning history: hover/focus reveals an exact day count. Week, month, and year are views of the same local record, not three competing dashboards.
  • Milestones: replace three floating sentences with small earned-insight chips, shown only when relevant.
  • Lessons: number each row, move CEFR to the end, and use pathway groupings plus one-line status instead of tall cards.
